What's ours vs. what's borrowed

Kept from reference (structural wins)

  • Dense patient header strip — this is just good information density, not a brand element
  • AI summary as contextual card within the active view — not a separate tab
  • Protocol steps as expandable cards with task progress

Our differentiators

  • Horizontal tabs — not a left sidebar. Cleaner, more modern, and doesn't eat horizontal space
  • KPI strip below header — CRS, care gaps, last contact, PDC, assignee all in one scannable row. No metadata sidebar.
  • AI action chips are specific — not generic "Schedule PCP" but contextual with clinical reasoning baked in
  • Active step auto-expands with inline task list — reference uses flat accordions
  • Overdue steps visually distinct — red border, not just a label
  • No separate assignee sidebar — integrated into KPI strip. Full width for content.
