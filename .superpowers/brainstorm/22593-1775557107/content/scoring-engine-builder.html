Scoring Engine Builder — How does an architect define a scoring rubric?

The scoring engine is optional. When enabled, it takes the cohort's matched patients and stratifies them into tiers by computing a weighted risk score. The diabetes CRS (5 components, weighted sum, tier thresholds) is one instance.

A

Table-Based Builder

A structured form. The architect adds scoring components as rows in a table. Each component expands to show its scoring rules (also a table of criterion → points). Weights are sliders/inputs at the component level. Tier thresholds are a separate table below.

Pros

  • Compact, data-dense — architects see everything at once
  • Familiar spreadsheet-like interaction
  • Easy to add/remove/reorder components

Cons

  • Can feel overwhelming with many rules
  • No visual sense of how components relate
B

Card-Based Builder

Each scoring component is a card. Cards show the component name, weight (as a visual bar), data source, and rule count. Click a card to open a drawer/panel where you configure that component's scoring rules. Tier thresholds shown as a visual gradient bar below the cards.

Pros

  • Visual weight representation at a glance
  • Less overwhelming — details are hidden until needed
  • Consistent with "card" pattern used elsewhere in the app

Cons

  • More clicks to see all rules (open each drawer)
  • Can't compare components side by side easily
C

Hybrid — Cards for Overview, Inline Expand for Detail

Components shown as cards (like B) but clicking a card expands it inline (accordion-style) to reveal the scoring rules table, rather than opening a separate drawer. You can have multiple components expanded at once to compare. Weight sliders live on the card header row.
