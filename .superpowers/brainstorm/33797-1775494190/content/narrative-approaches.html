Three Narrative Approaches for the Admin App

How should the app's information architecture tell the story? Pick the structure that best matches how you'd walk someone through it.

A

Pipeline Flow — Left to Right

The sidebar mirrors the care delivery pipeline in order: Data Ingestion → Cohortisation → Pathway Builder → Patient Registry → Care Delivery (AI Summary + Comms) → Outcomes

Pros

  • Tells the story linearly — "data comes in, patients get scored, pathways get built, care gets delivered, outcomes get measured"
  • Natural demo flow — walk top-to-bottom through the sidebar
  • Maps 1:1 to the document's module structure

Cons

  • Daily users (care managers) don't think in pipeline order — they think in tasks
  • Builder and Cohortisation are setup-once activities, not daily screens

Best for: When the primary goal is storytelling and the app is still more pitch than daily tool

B

Role-Based Workspaces

Two distinct workspace modes: Program Architect (Cohortisation config, Pathway Builder, Outcomes) and Care Manager (Patient Registry, AI Summary, Comms Agent). User switches between them.

Pros

  • Reflects real-world roles — clinical directors design programs, care managers execute them
  • Each workspace is focused and uncluttered
  • Scales naturally when more roles are added (pharmacist, CHW)

Cons

  • Two separate navigation trees to build and maintain
  • Demo requires switching contexts — harder to tell one linear story
  • More complex for a baseline app

Best for: When you want to demonstrate role-based access control and operational realism

C

Command Center + Deep Dives

A single AI-powered command center as the home screen — population health overview with live KPIs, AI-surfaced alerts, and intervention recommendations. Every other screen (Cohortisation, Builder, Patients, Comms, Outcomes) is a deep-dive accessible from the command center's cards and alerts.

Pros

  • Opens with impact — "here's your population, here's what needs attention NOW"
  • AI is front-and-center from the first screen, not buried in a tab
  • Natural drill-down: alert → patient → action → outcome
  • Demo story writes itself: "You log in. AI tells you 12 patients need attention. Click one. See the summary. Send the outreach. Done."

Cons

  • Command center is the most complex single screen to build
  • Builder and Cohortisation config feel less prominent (accessed via settings/config area)

Best for: When you want AI to be the headline capability and the app to feel like an intelligent system, not a dashboard

My recommendation: Option C. The biggest criticism was "no AI, insipid." A command center that opens with AI-driven insights immediately signals this is an intelligent platform. The builder and cohortisation engine become the "how it works" layer underneath — powerful when shown, but the daily experience leads with intelligence.
